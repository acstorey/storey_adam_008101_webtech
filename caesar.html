Caesar Cipher

Caesar

A Caesar cipher and the shift cipher, Caesar's code or also known as a Caesar shift, is one of the basic and most widely known encryption techniques. It is a type of substitution cipher in which each letter in plaintext is replaced by a letter some fixed number of positions down the alphabet, for an example with a left shift of 3, D would be replaced by A, E would then become B.
